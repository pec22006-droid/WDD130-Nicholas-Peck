Content

Home page

Welcome to Speedy Peck's guide to Minecraft Speedrunning! Here you will learn about the tips and tricks you will need to be able to complete your first speedrun on a random seed on Java edition. Individual speeds may vary, but this should be everything you need to know in order to achieve a sub-40 minute run.
Some tips and tricks:

  • Use version 1.16.1: they changed a lot about piglin trading and bastion remnants. It will make your life much easier to play on this version.
  • Change your gamma to 5. It is completely speedrun legal, and it makes it so that you can see underwater and in caves. This can be done by opening your .minecraft folder which by default can be found with windows key + R, and %appdata%/.minecraft on Windows or on Linux and Mac it can be found in your home folder
  • Use your sprint hotkey (by default Ctrl) to sprint rather than double tapping 'W'.
  • Use some hotkeys for your hotbar. Scrolling around is easier, but if you get used to using your hotkeys to navagate your hotbar, you can go much faster. (also, you should probably change at least the last three slots to something closer to your hand than 7, 8, and 9.)
A quick note before you start:
Minecraft speedrunning is HARD. You will fail a lot, and you will die a lot (in game, not in real life.) You will need patience, as you would when developing any other skill. The best runners have spent thousands of hours perfecting their craft. Don't expect to be an expert overnight. Don't expect to be an expert after just reading this. You will need some practice. without it you won't make much progress.

The Overworld

So, just starting out, what do we need before we go to the Nether? The answer is surprisingly not a lot! The short answer is we need 7 iron for a pickaxe, a bucket and a flint and steel, (don't forget the gravel to get the flint!) and we also need a bit of food. You can do this by going into caves to mine iron, and killing a few animals for food, however we will be covering how to use structures to make this easier and faster.
The first structure we will cover is villages. They vary in quality, but in almost all plains, desert, and savanna villages you can find a large quantity of hay bales. This can be your source of food, as well as a source of emeralds. Why would we want emeralds? for a strategy called "cod strats". It includes getting 3 emeralds, usually from trading wheat to a farmer villager (you can ensure a level 1 trade by destroying and replacing the composter until he gives you the desired trade.) After obtaining the three emeralds, you can trade them with a fisherman for a bucket of cod. This functions as the bucket, and the rest of the iron can be obtained by killing the iron golem. This can be done by pillaring up three blocks. He can't reach you from there. Alternatively, if you have a shovel, you can dig down two blocks.
It is also very useful to know what houses to check for loot. For beginners, I recommend taking a bed or two as they will help us out in the last step. Other than that, the most important loot can be found in the blacksmith house. This is the one with lava. It also has either cobblestone or diorite. The chest there can contain iron or diamonds, potentially also a pickaxe or obsidian all useful things to have.
The second structure we will cover is the desert temple. Here, all you have to do is get in, go to the central section, and mine down on one of the blocks next to the center block, and mine the block under the pressure plate. You can then mine the TNT, pillar out of the small hole with a TNT, then light one, as shown in the picture, and jump back into the hole. This is the fastest way to loot all of the chests, and it also gets you some cobblestone to make the rest of your tools out of. This is usually enough to get you enough blocks for your run, and your seven iron, and a bunch of rotten flesh that can be used as food, as well as often a golden apple to eat in case things ever get dicey.
The last structure we will cover are shipwrecks. There are three chests in shipwrecks. In the back there is a valuables chest. This will almost always have your seven iron, and enough extra iron, gold, or diamonds to make the rest of your tools. On the lower layer there are two chests. In the back, below the valuables chest is a chest that has a buried treasure map. We will not worry about this. It can be useful if the valuables chest doesn't have enough iron to make everything you need. In the front you can find the food chest. grab all of the wheat and carrots and suspicious stew from there. After looting everything, you can craft all of your tools, and the wheat into bread From there, it's usually recommended that you enter the nether through a magma ravine.
Now that you have your iron and food from your desired method, we've got to enter the nether. This can be done by using the mechanic that lava placed next to water will turn into obsidian. The two videos below show you how to make the portals, each one has one slow and one fast example.
A couple of notes about each one: for the surface lava version, you need to make sure that you start in the middle of a line of four lava. For the magma ravine version, you need to make sure have a setup like I do in the video with an "L" of magma. If you don't have obsidian on the sides, you will need to place lava there to complete the portal. Follow the video, then if you have any excess magma in the portal frame, you can get s bit of lava with your bucket, then mine it, and place the lava, then grab some water, and place it on the top of the inside of the frame

The Nether

So, you've made it to the nether. Now what? Now you need two things: ender pearls, and blaze rods. By far the easiest way to get ender pearls in verision 1.16.1 is by finding a bastion, and bartering with the piglins. The reason you want this version, is because in version 1.16.2, they changed the rates, and you get on average about 4 times fewer pearls from bartering.
So, how do you even find a bastion? Well, you could wander around aimlessly to find one, but that could take a while. The trick you need is called E-ray. What you do, is you lower your FOV.
FOV stands for field of view. With a lower number, it's like zooming in the camera that you can imagine is placed at your eyes so that you can see. Now, press

Yeah... That's a lot of information, but up there close to the top left at about the 5th line it has the letter E and then two numbers divided by a slash. The second one of those numbers is the number of entities (mobs, items, etc.) that are loaded in. The first number is how many of those are in your field of view, even counting ones that you can't see because they are through blocks.
Now, the important part: bastions generate with their mobs. If the second number is significantly more than 100 (something in the range of 130 to 180 usually) you have a bastion loaded. If you don't have a numeber that high, increase your render distance to at least 20 (the higher, the better) and wait a bit for a bastion to load in. Then, with your FOV lowered to 30, look around, and find where the first number spikes up to at least 30. If you get no "spike" in any direction, try increasing your entity distance to 500% (this one is also in the same page as render distance) and if you still don't see a spike, increase your render distance by about 2, and you should be able to find a spike.
Now that you have the spike, you just need to go in that direction, and like magic, there's a bastion!
Now that you're at the bastion, how do you properly get all of the gold to be able to barter with the piglins? Well in order to know that, we've got to know a little bit about bastions.
There are four types of bastions: Bridge, Stables, Treasure, and Housing. Each one has a slightly different way that you need to get all of the gold. I will be showing where the gold and showing one beginner route to be able to trade the gold with the piglins for each bastion type.
